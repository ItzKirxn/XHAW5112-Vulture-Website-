Program Overview

Our Garden Maintenance Training Program offers practical knowledge and hands-on experience to help you keep your garden in top condition throughout the year. Designed for both beginners and those looking to refine their skills, this program covers essential techniques and best practices for maintaining a healthy and beautiful garden.

Course Highlights:

  1. Garden Basics: Tools, Techniques, and Seasonal Care
    • Overview: Get started with the fundamentals of garden maintenance, including essential tools and basic techniques for year-round care.
    • Course Highlights:
      • Introduction to garden tools and equipment: pruning shears, spades, rakes, and more.
      • Basic gardening techniques: planting, weeding, and mulching.
      • Understanding seasonal garden care: spring, summer, fall, and winter tasks.
      • Soil preparation and basic fertilization methods.
    • Hands-On Project: Perform basic garden maintenance tasks and learn to use essential tools effectively.
    • Who Should Enroll: Beginners and homeowners who want to establish a solid foundation in garden maintenance.
  2. Pruning and Plant Care
    • Overview: Learn the art and science of pruning and caring for various types of plants to promote healthy growth and blooming.
    • Course Highlights:
      • Techniques for pruning shrubs, trees, and flowering plants.
      • Understanding plant growth cycles and seasonal pruning needs.
      • Identifying and managing common plant pests and diseases.
      • Methods for nurturing plants: watering, feeding, and supporting.
    • Hands-On Project: Prune a selection of plants and apply plant care techniques to enhance their health and appearance.
    • Who Should Enroll: Gardeners looking to improve their plant care skills and learn effective pruning methods.
  3. Lawn Care and Maintenance
    • Overview: Focus on the specific needs of lawn care to achieve a lush, green, and well-maintained lawn.
    • Course Highlights:
      • Basics of lawn care: mowing, edging, and fertilization.
      • Techniques for controlling weeds and pests in lawns.
      • Understanding different types of grass and their maintenance needs.
      • Seasonal lawn care: preparing for and dealing with winter, spring, summer, and fall.
    • Hands-On Project: Perform lawn maintenance tasks and implement techniques for a healthier, more vibrant lawn.
    • Who Should Enroll: Anyone responsible for lawn care or interested in improving their lawn maintenance practices.
  4. Irrigation and Water Management
    • Overview: Learn effective irrigation techniques and water management strategies to keep your garden hydrated and healthy.
    • Course Highlights:
      • Overview of irrigation systems: sprinklers, drip systems, and soaker hoses.
      • Techniques for efficient water use and conservation.
      • Designing and installing irrigation systems for different garden areas.
      • Troubleshooting and maintaining irrigation equipment.
    • Hands-On Project: Design and install a basic irrigation system for a garden area and practice efficient watering techniques.
    • Who Should Enroll: Gardeners interested in improving their irrigation practices and ensuring efficient water use.
  5. Composting and Soil Health
    • Overview: Explore composting methods and soil health practices to enhance garden fertility and sustainability.
    • Course Highlights:
      • Basics of composting: types of composters, materials, and processes.
      • Techniques for creating and maintaining healthy compost.
      • Understanding soil health: testing, amending, and improving soil quality.
      • Benefits of composting and using organic matter in the garden.
    • Hands-On Project: Create a compost bin and begin composting, while testing and amending soil for better garden health.
    • Who Should Enroll: Those interested in sustainable gardening practices and improving soil quality through composting.
  6. Seasonal Garden Planning and Management
    • Overview: Learn how to plan and manage your garden throughout the year, including seasonal adjustments and long-term planning.
    • Course Highlights:
      • Planning garden tasks and projects according to the seasons.
      • Strategies for rotating crops and managing garden beds.
      • Preparing for seasonal changes: planting schedules and seasonal crops.
      • Long-term garden management and design considerations.
    • Hands-On Project: Develop a seasonal garden plan and implement strategies for ongoing management and adjustment.
    • Who Should Enroll: Gardeners who want to optimize their garden management and planning skills throughout the year.

  • Hands-On Learning: Engage in practical exercises and real-life garden maintenance tasks.

  • Flexible Scheduling: Classes available at various times to fit your schedule.
