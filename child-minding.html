Program Overview

Our Child-Minding Training Program is designed to provide caregivers, parents, and professionals with the skills and knowledge needed to ensure the well-being, safety, and development of children. Over the course of several weeks, participants will gain practical experience and theoretical understanding in child care, development, and emergency response.

Course Highlights:

  1. Introduction to Child-Minding: Roles and Responsibilities
    • Overview: This foundational class introduces the core responsibilities and best practices of child-minding, setting the stage for effective and responsible caregiving.
    • Course Highlights:
      • Overview of the child-minding role: duties and expectations.
      • Understanding child development stages and their impact on care.
      • Creating a safe and nurturing environment for children.
      • Effective communication with children and parents.
    • Hands-On Project: Develop a basic child-minding plan that includes daily routines, activities, and safety measures.
    • Who Should Enroll: New caregivers, parents, and individuals interested in learning the fundamentals of child-minding.
  2. Child Safety and First Aid
    • Overview: Focus on essential safety practices and first aid skills necessary for responding to emergencies and maintaining a safe environment for children.
    • Course Highlights:
      • Child safety: home safety checks, preventing accidents, and child-proofing.
      • Basic first aid skills: CPR for children, managing cuts, burns, and choking.
      • Recognizing and responding to signs of illness and injury.
      • Emergency procedures and contacting emergency services.
    • Hands-On Project: Participate in first aid simulations and safety drills to practice emergency response skills.
    • Who Should Enroll: Caregivers and parents seeking to enhance their safety and first aid skills in child care settings.
  3. Nutrition and Healthy Eating for Children
    • Overview: Learn about the nutritional needs of children and how to plan and prepare healthy, balanced meals.
    • Course Highlights:
      • Basics of child nutrition: essential nutrients and dietary requirements.
      • Planning balanced meals and snacks for different age groups.
      • Addressing common dietary concerns: allergies, picky eaters, and special diets.
      • Safe food preparation and hygiene practices.
    • Hands-On Project: Plan and prepare a week's worth of nutritious meals and snacks tailored for different age groups.
    • Who Should Enroll: Caregivers and parents looking to understand and implement healthy eating practices for children.
  4. Engaging Activities and Developmental Play
    • Overview: Explore age-appropriate activities and play strategies that support children's development and learning.
    • Course Highlights:
      • Understanding the benefits of play in child development.
      • Designing and implementing creative activities for various age groups.
      • Encouraging social, emotional, and cognitive development through play.
      • Incorporating educational toys and resources.
    • Hands-On Project: Create a set of engaging and developmentally appropriate activities and lesson plans for children.
    • Who Should Enroll: Caregivers and educators interested in enhancing their ability to engage children in meaningful play and learning activities.
  5. Behavior Management and Positive Discipline
    • Overview: Learn strategies for managing behavior and implementing positive discipline techniques to foster a supportive and structured environment.
    • Course Highlights:
      • Understanding child behavior and common challenges.
      • Techniques for positive discipline and setting clear boundaries.
      • Strategies for managing challenging behaviors and conflicts.
      • Building a supportive and respectful relationship with children.
    • Hands-On Project: Develop a behavior management plan with strategies for positive discipline and behavior support.
    • Who Should Enroll: Caregivers and parents looking to improve their skills in managing behavior and implementing effective discipline techniques.
  6. Communication with Parents and Professionals
    • Overview: This class focuses on effective communication strategies with parents and other professionals involved in child care.

      • Handling sensitive topics and providing constructive feedback.
      • Documentation and reporting: maintaining records and communicating observations.
    • Hands-On Project: Role-play various scenarios of communication with parents and professionals, and create a communication plan.
    • Who Should Enroll: Caregivers and child care professionals seeking to improve their communication skills with parents and colleagues.
