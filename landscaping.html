Program Overview

Our Landscaping Training Program offers a series of classes designed to equip participants with the skills and knowledge needed to create and maintain beautiful, functional outdoor spaces. Whether you're a homeowner looking to enhance your garden or an aspiring professional landscaper, our program covers a range of topics from basic design principles to advanced horticultural techniques.

Course Highlights:

  1. Introduction to Landscaping: Fundamentals and Design
    • Overview: This introductory class covers the essential principles of landscaping, including design basics and planning.
    • Course Highlights:
      • Fundamentals of landscape design: concepts, terminology, and styles.
      • Understanding soil types, drainage, and environmental factors.
      • Introduction to plant selection and placement.
      • Hands-on project: designing a basic garden layout.
    • Who Should Enroll: Beginners and homeowners interested in learning the basics of landscaping and garden design.
  2. Planting and Garden Maintenance
    • Overview: Focus on the practical aspects of planting and maintaining a garden to ensure healthy, thriving plants.
    • Course Highlights:
      • Techniques for planting and transplanting various types of plants.
      • Soil preparation, fertilization, and irrigation practices.
      • Seasonal garden maintenance tasks: pruning, weeding, and pest control.
      • Hands-on project: creating and maintaining a small garden bed.
    • Who Should Enroll: Gardening enthusiasts and those looking to improve their garden maintenance skills.
  3. Hardscaping: Structures and Materials
    • Overview: Learn about the design and installation of hardscape elements such as patios, walkways, and retaining walls.
    • Course Highlights:
      • Overview of hardscaping materials: stone, brick, pavers, and concrete.
      • Design principles for integrating hardscape features with landscapes.
      • Installation techniques for patios, walkways, and retaining walls.
      • Hands-on project: designing and installing a small hardscape feature.
    • Who Should Enroll: Those interested in adding durable, aesthetic elements to their outdoor spaces, including aspiring hardscape professionals.
  4. Sustainable Landscaping: Eco-Friendly Practices
    • Overview: Explore sustainable landscaping practices that promote environmental health and conservation.
    • Course Highlights:
      • Principles of sustainable landscaping: reducing water usage, native plants, and soil conservation.
      • Techniques for creating eco-friendly landscapes: rain gardens, xeriscaping, and composting.
      • Managing pests and diseases organically.
      • Hands-on project: designing a sustainable garden plan.
    • Who Should Enroll: Homeowners and landscapers interested in eco-friendly practices and creating environmentally responsible landscapes.
  5. Advanced Landscape Design and Planning
    • Overview: This advanced class delves into complex landscape design techniques and project planning.
    • Course Highlights:
      • Advanced design principles: spatial design, focal points, and thematic elements.
      • Developing detailed landscape plans and specifications.
      • Project management: budgeting, scheduling, and client communication.
      • Hands-on project: creating a comprehensive landscape design plan.
    • Who Should Enroll: Experienced landscapers and individuals seeking to refine their design skills and manage larger projects.
  6. Specialty Landscaping: Water Features and Outdoor Lighting
    • Overview: Focus on creating and integrating water features and outdoor lighting into landscape designs.
    • Course Highlights:
      • Design and installation of water features: ponds, fountains, and waterfalls.
      • Principles of outdoor lighting: types of lights, placement, and effects.
      • Maintenance and troubleshooting for water features and lighting systems.
      • Hands-on project: installing a small water feature and outdoor lighting setup.
    • Who Should Enroll: Those interested in adding decorative and functional elements to their landscapes, including professionals and avid gardeners.

  • Practical Experience: Gain hands-on experience through real-life projects and practical applications.
